Exponent Rules
The 3 Main Rules
Master these three first. Everything else builds on them.
✖️
Multiplying
Product Rule
aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ
When multiplying same base, ADD the exponents.

x³ × x⁵ = x⁸
3 + 5 = 8

2⁴ × 2³ = 2⁷ = 128
⚡ Key: Bases must be the SAME. You cannot use this rule on x² × y³.
➗
Dividing
Quotient Rule
aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ
When dividing same base, SUBTRACT the exponents.

x⁷ ÷ x² = x⁵
7 - 2 = 5

5⁶ ÷ 5² = 5⁴ = 625
⚡ Always top exponent MINUS bottom exponent.
🔢
Power of Power
Power Rule
(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐˣⁿ
When raising a power to another power, MULTIPLY.

(x³)⁴ = x¹²
3 × 4 = 12

(2²)³ = 2⁶ = 64
⚡ Power OUTSIDE applies to EVERYTHING inside the parentheses.
Special Rules
Zero & Negative Exponents
These come up constantly — memorize both before Friday.
a⁰ = 1
Zero Exponent Rule
Any non-zero number raised to the power of 0 equals 1. Always. No exceptions (except 0⁰ which is undefined).
5⁰ = 1  ·  x⁰ = 1  ·  (2y)⁰ = 1  ·  1000⁰ = 1
a⁻ᵇ = 1/aᵇ
Negative Exponent Rule
A negative exponent means take the reciprocal (flip it). The exponent itself becomes positive once flipped.
x⁻³ = 1/x³  ·  2⁻⁴ = 1/16  ·  5⁻² = 1/25

Scientific Notation
Writing & Computing
Used to write very large or very small numbers in a clean, standard form.
Standard Form
a × 10n
The "a" part
Must be: 1 ≤ a < 10
A number with ONE digit before the decimal point
The "n" part (exponent)
Positive = BIG number
Negative = SMALL (decimal) number
Large Number
5,300,000
= 5.3 × 10⁶
Move decimal LEFT 6 places
Small Number
0.000047
= 4.7 × 10⁻⁵
Move decimal RIGHT 5 places
Back to Standard
3.2 × 10⁴
= 32,000
Move decimal RIGHT 4 places
🔢 Computing with Scientific Notation
Four operations — here is exactly what to do for each one.
OperationMethodExampleAnswer
Multiply Multiply the "a" parts.
ADD the exponents.		 (3 × 10²) × (2 × 10⁴) 6 × 10⁶
Divide Divide the "a" parts.
SUBTRACT the exponents.		 (8 × 10⁵) ÷ (4 × 10²) 2 × 10³
Add Make exponents EQUAL first.
Then add the "a" parts.		 (3 × 10⁴) + (2 × 10³) 3.2 × 10⁴
Subtract Make exponents EQUAL first.
Then subtract the "a" parts.		 (5 × 10⁶) - (3 × 10⁵) 4.7 × 10⁶
💡 Decimal Direction Trick — Never Forget
Standard → Scientific Notation
Big number (≥10): move decimal LEFT → exponent is positive
Small number (<1): move decimal RIGHT → exponent is negative
Scientific → Standard Notation
Positive exponent: move decimal RIGHT (bigger)
Negative exponent: move decimal LEFT (smaller)
